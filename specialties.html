Specialties

Focused expertise for uncommon challenges

Primary Areas of Focus

These are the areas where I have the deepest expertise and can make the greatest impact for your family.

Child Therapy

Children aren't small adults. They communicate, process emotions, and heal differently than adults do. My approach to child therapy uses interventions designed specifically for how children think and feel—including play therapy, sandtray, and expressive arts.

I work with children ages 5-17 on issues including:

  • Anxiety and fears
  • Behavioral issues
  • ADHD
  • School difficulties
  • Anger and aggression
  • Social skills challenges
  • Adjustment to divorce or family changes

Trauma & PTSD

Trauma affects children and adults differently. I provide evidence-based trauma treatment including EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) and trauma-focused therapy approaches adapted for children's developmental needs.

I help children and families heal from:

  • Abuse and neglect
  • Witnessing domestic violence
  • Accidents or medical trauma
  • Loss and grief
  • Attachment disruptions
  • Complex trauma histories

Parenting Support

Parents are partners in their child's therapy—not observers from the sidelines. I help parents understand what's happening with their child and give immediate, actionable strategies they can use right away.

"Putting Parents Back in Charge" is more than a tagline—it's my approach. Many families come to me feeling like they've lost control of their household. I help you regain that sense of confidence and competence as a parent.

I work with families struggling with:

  • Defiant behavior
  • Power struggles
  • Co-parenting challenges
  • Blended family dynamics
  • Single parenting
  • Parent-child communication breakdowns

Childhood Chronic Constipation

One of the only therapists in Louisville specializing in this condition.

Chronic constipation in children (also known as encopresis when it involves soiling) is far more common than most people realize—and it's almost always connected to behavioral, emotional, or psychological factors. That's why therapists can diagnose and treat it.

If your child has been on the "MiraLAX merry-go-round" with no lasting improvement, it may be time to address the underlying causes rather than just the symptoms.

I help children and families with:

  • Chronic constipation with behavioral components
  • Encopresis (soiling)
  • Toilet training resistance
  • Anxiety around bathroom use
  • Medical-behavioral coordination

Why Child-Specific Training Matters

Not all therapists are trained specifically to work with children. Adult therapy approaches don't simply "scale down" to work with kids—children require fundamentally different methods.

When working with children, therapists need specialized training in:

  • Developmental psychology — Understanding how children think and feel at different ages
  • Play-based interventions — Meeting children where they are communicatively
  • Family systems — Working with the whole family, not just the child in isolation
  • Parent coaching — Equipping parents with skills they can use at home
  • Child-adapted trauma treatment — Trauma affects children differently than adults

My 19 years of experience have been focused specifically on children, teens, and families—not general adult therapy with occasional child clients.

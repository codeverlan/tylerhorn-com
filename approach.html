Therapeutic Approach

Evidence-based methods designed for children

Child-Centered, Parent-Involved

The interventions I use aren't adult therapies adapted for children—they're approaches designed from the ground up for how children think, communicate, and process emotions. At the same time, parents are active partners in the therapeutic process, not observers from the waiting room.

Core Therapeutic Methods

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)

EMDR is an evidence-based treatment for trauma and PTSD that helps the brain process difficult memories and experiences. It's been extensively researched and is endorsed by the World Health Organization for trauma treatment.

For children, EMDR is adapted to be developmentally appropriate. Children don't need to verbally recount every detail of their trauma—the therapy works by engaging the brain's natural healing processes.

Effective for: Trauma, PTSD, anxiety, phobias, nightmares, distressing memories

Play Therapy & Sandtray

Play is a child's natural language. Through play therapy, children can express feelings, process experiences, and develop coping skills in ways that feel natural and safe to them.

Sandtray therapy involves using miniature figures in a tray of sand to create scenes that represent the child's inner world. This non-verbal approach is especially powerful for children who struggle to put their experiences into words.

Effective for: Anxiety, behavioral issues, trauma, emotional regulation, social skills, family transitions

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps children (and adults) understand the connection between their thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. By identifying and challenging unhelpful thought patterns, we can change how they feel and act.

For children, CBT is made concrete and engaging—using games, activities, and visual tools rather than abstract discussion.

Effective for: Anxiety, depression, anger management, behavioral issues, OCD, phobias

Attachment-Based Therapy

Healthy attachment between children and caregivers is the foundation for emotional wellbeing. When attachment is disrupted—through trauma, adoption, foster care, or other circumstances—children may struggle with trust, relationships, and emotional regulation.

Child-Parent Relational Therapy and other attachment-focused approaches help repair and strengthen the parent-child bond.

Effective for: Attachment difficulties, adoption/foster care transitions, trauma recovery, behavioral issues, parent-child conflict

Art & Expressive Therapies

Art therapy and other expressive approaches give children another avenue for processing emotions and experiences. Drawing, painting, and creative activities can be especially helpful for children who are resistant to traditional "talk therapy."

Effective for: Trauma, emotional expression, anxiety, depression, building coping skills

Additional Approaches

I draw from multiple modalities based on what will work best for each child and family.

Trauma-Focused CBT

Specialized CBT approach for children who have experienced trauma.

Family Systems

Understanding and addressing dynamics within the whole family.

Solution-Focused Brief Therapy

Goal-oriented approach focused on solutions rather than problems.

Polyvagal-Informed

Understanding the nervous system's role in emotional regulation.

Motivational Interviewing

Helping clients find their own motivation for change.

Coaching

Practical guidance and strategies for parents and families.

What to Expect

A Direct Approach

I'm often the therapist parents find after trying others with a less direct approach. I believe in giving immediate, actionable feedback and strategies—not vague observations or endless exploration without guidance.

After our sessions, you'll know exactly what to do next. I'll tell you what I see, what I think it means, and what we're going to do about it.

Parent Involvement

Parents are active participants in their child's therapy, not observers in the waiting room. I'll teach you skills and strategies you can use at home, and keep you informed about what we're working on and why.

You're the expert on your child—I'm here to partner with you, not to take over.

Questions About My Approach?

I'm happy to discuss which methods might be most helpful for your situation.
